## Setting Up Your WIZIUM Workspace

Getting started with WIZIUM is straightforward and takes just a few minutes. This guide will walk you through creating your account, configuring your workspace, and importing your first documents. By the end, you'll have a fully functional documentation environment ready for your team.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create Account" icon="user">
    Visit wizium.com/signup and provide your email address and a secure password. You'll receive a verification email to confirm your account.

    <Callout kind="info">Use a business email for team collaboration features.</Callout>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify Email" icon="mail">
    Check your inbox for the verification link and click it to activate your account. This step ensures account security.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set Up Workspace" icon="folder">
    Choose a workspace name that reflects your project or organization. You can create multiple workspaces for different teams or projects.

    <CodeGroup tabs="Web Interface,API">
      ````javascript
      // Web Interface: Click "Create Workspace" in dashboard
      // Fill in: Name, Description, Team Size
      ````
      ````bash
      curl -X POST "https://api.wizium.com/v1/workspaces" \
        -H "Authorization: Bearer YOUR_API_KEY" \
        -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
        -d '{"name": "My Project", "description": "Documentation workspace"}'
      ````
    </CodeGroup>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Invite Team Members" icon="users">
    Add colleagues by entering their email addresses. Assign roles like Admin, Editor, or Viewer based on their needs.

    <Expandable title="Role Permissions" default-open="false">
      - **Admin**: Full access to settings and billing
      - **Editor**: Can create and edit documents
      - **Viewer**: Read-only access to published docs
    </Expandable>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Import Documents" icon="upload">
    Upload existing documentation in various formats including Markdown, HTML, and PDF. WIZIUM will automatically convert them.

    <Tabs>
      <Tab title="Markdown" icon="file-text">
        ````bash
        # Example: Import a README.md file
        curl -X POST "https://api.wizium.com/v1/documents/import" \
          -H "Authorization: Bearer YOUR_API_KEY" \
          -F "file=@README.md" \
          -F "format=markdown"
        ````
      </Tab>
      <Tab title="HTML" icon="code">
        ````bash
        # Import HTML documentation
        curl -X POST "https://api.wizium.com/v1/documents/import" \
          -H "Authorization: Bearer YOUR_API_KEY" \
          -F "file=@docs.html" \
          -F "format=html"
        ````
      </Tab>
    </Tabs>
  </Step>
</Steps>

<ExpandableGroup>

  </Expandable>
  <Expandable title="Access Controls" default-open="false">

  </Expandable>

  </Expandable>

## Best Practices for Setup

<Callout kind="tip">Start small with a pilot project to test WIZIUM's capabilities before rolling out to your entire organization.</Callout>

Consider these recommendations for a successful WIZIUM implementation:

- Begin with 2-3 team members to establish workflows
- Import a representative sample of your existing documentation
- Set up clear naming conventions for documents and folders
- Configure notifications to keep team members informed of updates
- Enable version history to track changes over time

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

If you encounter problems during setup, check our troubleshooting guide or contact support.

| Issue | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| Email verification not received | Check spam folder, resend verification email |
| Workspace creation fails | Ensure workspace name is unique and under 50 characters |
| Import errors | Verify file format is supported (MD, HTML, PDF) |
| Team invites not working | Confirm email addresses are valid and not blocked |
